UV Spectrophotometric Method Development for Metformin and Glimepiride
About the Project

This repository contains my undergraduate research project completed during my Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm). The project focuses on developing a simple, rapid, and cost-effective UV spectrophotometric method for the analysis of Metformin Hydrochloride and Glimepiride, two widely prescribed antidiabetic drugs.

The work explores the analytical behavior of both drugs, evaluates the possibility of simultaneous estimation using UV spectroscopy, and discusses an alternative approach for improving Glimepiride detection.

Objectives
Determine the λmax of Metformin and Glimepiride
Prepare standard and working solutions
Develop a calibration curve for Metformin
Verify Beer-Lambert's Law
Study spectral characteristics of both drugs
Evaluate the limitations of simultaneous estimation
Propose Solid Phase Extraction (SPE) as an alternative method

Key Findings
Metformin showed a clear absorbance maximum around 236 nm.
The calibration curve demonstrated excellent linearity within the selected concentration range.
Due to its very low concentration and weak absorbance, Glimepiride could not be accurately estimated simultaneously using the developed UV method.
Solid Phase Extraction (SPE) was proposed to improve the selective estimation of Glimepiride.
